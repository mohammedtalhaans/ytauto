# Hedge Funds in 30 Seconds

A clean 3D infographic explains hedge funds through a single glowing briefcase that transforms into investors, long-short trades, leverage, and the 2-and-20 fee split.

## Narration script (~94 words, TTS via edge-tts)

> A hedge fund is a private investment pool, usually for wealthy or professional investors. The manager tries to beat the market using flexible strategies. They can buy assets they expect to rise, short assets they expect to fall, and sometimes borrow money to amplify bets. That can boost gains, but it also raises risk. Hedge funds make money in two ways: returns for investors, and fees for the manager. The famous model is two and twenty: about two percent of assets each year, plus twenty percent of profits. Powerful tool? Yes. Simple magic? No.

## Opening hook (first 3-5s of segment 1)

> At 0s, a matte-black briefcase snaps open in extreme close-up and releases a vertical fountain of glowing gold coins and navy glass bars that freeze mid-air, then orbit into a floating miniature financial machine.
