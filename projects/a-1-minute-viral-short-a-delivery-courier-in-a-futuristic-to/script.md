# 60 Seconds to Shibuya Zero

A futuristic Tokyo courier finds a time-bending package in her bag and has one minute to deliver it before Shibuya collapses into a permanent frozen moment.

## Opening hook (first 3-5s of segment 1)

> At 0s, a glowing black cube bursts out of Mika's courier bag in extreme close-up, slams into wet asphalt, and makes every raindrop, scooter, pedestrian, and neon reflection freeze mid-motion except Mika, while a red 60-second countdown ignites across the cube's glass seams.

## Style bible

- **Era**: near-future cyberpunk thriller with contemporary 2020s kinetic short-film realism
- **Lens**: 28mm anamorphic for vertical action, shallow depth of field on faces and props, deeper focus during sprinting wide shots
- **Grade**: high-contrast neon cyan-magenta with amber highlights, wet reflective blacks, soft bloom around holograms
- **Stock**: digital cinema with subtle film grain emulation, controlled halation on neon and rain reflections
- **Motion**: kinetic handheld chase energy with whip-pans, crash zooms on the countdown, fast rack-focuses between Mika and the chrono-cube

## Audio bible

- **Music**: driving cyberpunk taiko percussion plus analog synth bass, 128 bpm, urgent five-note motif in F minor that accelerates and swells in segment 4
- **Ambient**: continuous rainy Tokyo night bed, distant traffic wash, electric billboard hum, passing drones, muffled train rumbles
- **SFX motif**: the sharp crystalline tick of the chrono-cube countdown, recurring whenever time bends or the package changes hands
- **Mix**: music stays urgent and continuous, countdown ticks and time-freeze impacts punch forward, city rain and neon hum sit underneath

## Story beats

1. Segment 1 beat — the glowing chrono-cube erupts from Mika's bag, freezes a rain-soaked Shibuya crossing, and forces her to accept a 60-second delivery countdown.
2. Segment 2 beat — Mika sprints through a neon service alley as time glitches violently around her and a black-clad time enforcer begins hunting the cube.
3. Segment 3 beat — Mika reaches an abandoned elevated monorail platform, discovers the package is stabilizing the entire ward, and chooses the risky rooftop shortcut as the countdown turns critical.
4. Segment 4 beat — Mika crashes into the clock tower shrine, hands the chrono-cube to Ren, and the city unfreezes with a final twist: the package was sent by Mika herself from one minute in the future.

## Characters

- **mika-sato** — 27-year-old Japanese woman, warm light olive skin, amber-brown eyes, jet-black chin-length hair with blunt bangs, slightly wavy ends tucked behind one ear, wearing a matte charcoal armored courier jacket with cyan reflective piping, black compression undershirt, slim cargo riding pants, fingerless tactical gloves, and scuffed white high-top delivery sneakers; small crescent scar through her left eyebrow, silver ear cuff, alert forward-leaning runner posture.
- **ren-kurogane** — 41-year-old Japanese man, pale beige skin with faint grey undertone, dark brown eyes, shoulder-length salt-and-black straight hair tied in a low knot, wearing a long ivory technical lab coat over a black turtleneck, graphite utility trousers, polished black split-toe boots, and a transparent wrist console strapped over one sleeve; thin rectangular glasses, vertical burn mark along his right jaw, precise still posture.
- **black-enforcer** — 34-year-old Korean man, cool medium tan skin, steel-grey eyes, shaved black hair with a narrow center strip, wearing a glossy black armored pursuit coat with red LED seam lines, high-collar respirator mask, layered carbon-fiber chest rig, black tactical trousers, and magnetic black boots; red hourglass tattoo visible on the left side of his neck, rigid predatory posture, always framed as fast and angular.

## Settings

- **shibuya-rain-crossing** — futuristic Shibuya scramble crossing with layered pedestrian decks, holographic kanji billboards, wet black asphalt, vending drones, chrome traffic pylons, and glass storefronts. Night during heavy rain, lit by cyan-magenta neon and cold white street LEDs. Dominant palette: electric cyan, hot pink, glossy black.
- **neon-service-alley** — narrow Tokyo service alley between modular high-rises, exposed steam pipes, stacked delivery lockers, glowing noodle signs, puddled concrete, and tangled fiber-optic cables. Night lighting from red lanterns, blue maintenance strips, and flickering vending machines. Dominant palette: crimson, cobalt blue, oily silver.
- **monorail-rooftop-platform** — abandoned elevated monorail platform above Shibuya rooftops, cracked safety glass, rusted yellow railings, dead ticket gates, wind-torn ad banners, and distant skyline towers. Pre-dawn darkness with storm flashes and green emergency beacons. Dominant palette: storm grey, acid green, deep violet.
- **clock-tower-shrine** — tiny techno-shrine built into a rooftop clock tower, brass gears behind glass walls, red paper prayer strips, black stone floor, server racks, and a central circular delivery altar. Night tipping into dawn, lit by warm amber clocklight and blue reactor glow. Dominant palette: amber gold, reactor blue, lacquer black.

## Props

- **chrono-cube** — palm-sized black glass cube with beveled titanium edges, glowing cyan cracks, red digital countdown numerals floating inside, and tiny engraved text reading 'SHIBUYA ZERO'; usually held in Mika's gloved hand or hovering inches above a delivery surface.
- **mika-courier-bag** — compact matte charcoal crossbody delivery satchel with hard-shell corners, cyan reflective zipper track, magnetic latch, scratched white courier logo, and rain beads on the surface; usually slung tight across Mika's back or snapping open in her hands.
- **ren-wrist-console** — transparent curved wrist device with brushed silver frame, amber holographic clock rings, hairline cracks across the glass, and a pulsing blue receiver dot; usually strapped over Ren's coat sleeve or raised toward the chrono-cube.
