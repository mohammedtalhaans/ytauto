# Memory Blade

A cybernetic samurai assassin cuts through a holographic Tokyo penthouse to recover a stolen memory crystal before a corporate director uploads it into the city grid.

## Opening hook (first 3-5s of segment 1)

> At 0s, the stolen memory crystal spins inches from camera inside a glass display cube, then violently projects a full-size ghost of Mika's childhood memory into the penthouse air as alarms fracture the room into cyan holographic shards.

## Style bible

- **Era**: kinetic neo-noir cyberpunk action cinema with 1990s anime influence and modern luxury tech minimalism
- **Lens**: 28mm anamorphic for vertical action, shallow depth of field on close combat, deeper focus for skyline and corridor movement
- **Grade**: high-contrast cyan-magenta noir, crushed wet blacks, electric highlights, controlled red alarm accents
- **Stock**: digital cinema with crisp VFX detail, subtle film grain, neon halation, rain-slick specular bloom
- **Motion**: aggressive vertical handheld tracking, whip-pans between blade strikes, fast push-ins on props, brief slow-motion at impact moments

## Audio bible

- **Music**: driving industrial taiko + distorted synth bass, 142 bpm, tense five-note motif in F minor that accelerates into a final percussive swell
- **Ambient**: continuous rainy Tokyo rooftop hum, distant traffic far below, electrical transformer buzz, muffled thunder through glass
- **SFX motif**: the crystalline three-note chime of the memory crystal, recurring whenever it moves, pulses, or changes hands
- **Mix**: music relentless and cohesive, blade impacts and crystal chimes sharp in foreground, city rain and electrical ambience underneath

## Story beats

1. Segment 1: The memory crystal's sudden holographic eruption exposes the theft and reveals Mika already inside the penthouse, forcing her to strike before the alarm locks down.
2. Segment 2: Mika cuts through the atrium guard line with the plasma katana, proving the mission is personal as the crystal is rushed deeper into the tower.
3. Segment 3: The guards trap Mika in a gravity-distorted corridor, but she adapts her cybernetics, reverses their field, and breaks into the memory vault.
4. Segment 4: Mika confronts Director Saito, seizes the crystal during its upload, and escapes through the shattered skyline as her stolen memory returns.

## Planned spectacle / VFX moments

- Segment 1: the memory crystal detonates a 3-metre cyan hologram burst that turns the penthouse walls transparent, exposing Tokyo far below through floating glass-like data shards.
- Segment 2: Mika's plasma katana carves a magenta arc through three rifle drones, their severed halves hovering for half a second before raining sparks downward.
- Segment 3: the onyx-guards fire synchronized gravity rounds that pin furniture, rain, and shattered marble fragments sideways across the corridor while Mika runs along the wall.
- Segment 4: the memory crystal overloads in Mika's palm, releasing a controlled cyclone of cyan particles that rebuilds the stolen memory around her as the penthouse windows explode outward in slow motion.

## Characters

- **mika-kurogane** — 31yo Japanese woman, pale warm skin, steel-grey eyes, black straight shoulder-length hair in high half-knot; matte black segmented samurai armor over charcoal bodysuit, armored tabi boots; chrome left forearm, thin scar under right eye, still predatory posture.
- **director-saito** — 46yo Japanese man, cool beige skin, amber-brown eyes, silver-black slicked-back short hair; ivory tech-weave suit, black collarless shirt, polished graphite shoes; gold neural ports along temples, immaculate posture, faint surgical smile.
- **onyx-guards** — 32yo Japanese-Korean clone enforcers, medium tan skin, dark brown eyes, black buzz-cut hair under visored helmets; glossy onyx tactical armor, grey undersuits, magnetic boots; identical jaw tattoos, rigid synchronized stance, compact pulse rifles.

## Settings

- **holographic-penthouse-atrium** — triple-height Tokyo penthouse atrium with minimalist corporate architecture, black marble floor, floating koi holograms, glass display cube, sunken lounge, chrome bonsai sculpture. Midnight rain outside, cyan and magenta hologram light over obsidian, electric blue, polished silver.
- **neon-skybridge-hall** — narrow glass skybridge hallway suspended above Tokyo, angular steel ribs, reflective floor panels, abstract ad projections, ceiling mist vents, security drone rails. Night city glow and cold white strip lights create violet, cyan, and wet-black reflections.
- **gravity-security-corridor** — long penthouse security corridor in brutalist luxury style, ribbed concrete walls, levitating marble benches, broken mirror panels, weapon lockers, red sensor lasers. Emergency strobes and rain-lit windows cast crimson, gunmetal, and ice-blue light.
- **memory-vault-terrace** — open-air rooftop vault terrace with circular carbon-fiber upload dais, transparent blast screens, bonsai planters, antenna spires, shallow rainwater pooling on black stone. Stormy pre-dawn skyline glows cyan, violet, and pale silver.

## Props

- **memory-crystal** — thumb-sized faceted crystal of translucent cyan glass, hexagonal teardrop shape, hairline gold circuitry suspended inside, one chipped corner pulsing brighter than the rest; usually floating in a display field or clenched in Mika's cybernetic palm.
- **plasma-katana** — black carbon katana hilt with brushed titanium guard, blade ignites as a metre-long magenta plasma edge with a white-hot core and violet afterimage; usually drawn from Mika's back sheath or cutting luminous arcs through frame.
- **fox-infiltration-mask** — matte white cyber-fox half-mask with hairline cracks, black ceramic cheek vents, tiny red optical sensors, and a faint chrome mouth seam; usually clipped to Mika's face during stealth, then hanging at her collar during combat.
